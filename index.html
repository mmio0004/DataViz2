Understanding Cardiovascular Diseases

Cardiovascular diseases (CVDs) are the leading cause of death globally. This visualisation dashboard explores the complex relationships between economic factors, risk elements, and death rates caused by CVDs across different countries and continents in 2021.

Who: Public health researchers, policymakers, general public.
What: Key visual insights into CVD death rates and risk factors.
Why: To identify potential interventions and understand disparities in health outcomes.
How: By visualizing the data from a global perspective and interpreting the results clearly.

Cardiovascular Death Rate and GDP

This bubble chart demonstrates the relationship between GDP per capita and cardiovascular death rates across countries. Countries with higher GDPs often show lower death rates from CVDs.

Annotation: Hover over each bubble to see specific country data.

Cardiovascular Death Rate by Continent

This lollipop chart visualizes the death rates due to CVDs across different continents. Africa and Europe demonstrate significant variances in death rates compared to other regions.

Annotation: Hover on the lollipops for precise death rates.

Cardiovascular Death Rate Map

A geographic map highlighting the CVD death rates across the globe in 2021. Darker regions indicate higher death rates, allowing a quick visual assessment of which countries are most affected.

Risk Factors for Cardiovascular Deaths

This Sankey diagram demonstrates how various risk factors (e.g., smoking, high cholesterol, high blood pressure) contribute to the global death toll from CVDs in 2021.

Deaths by Cardiovascular Disease Type

This sunburst chart categorizes the leading causes of death from CVDs, focusing on the top contributors such as coronary heart disease and stroke. The outer layers illustrate the contribution of different regions to these figures.
