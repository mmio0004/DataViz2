Global Death Rate from Cardiovascular Diseases

In 2021, Nauru (694.26), the Solomon Islands (566.07) and Uzbekistan (512.86) had the highest cardiovascular death rates, driven by smoking, poor diet and limited healthcare. In contrast, San Marino (60.13), South Korea (60.42) and Japan (69.25) saw the lowest rates, due to strong healthcare systems and healthier lifestyles.

Ranking of Continents by Cardiovascular Disease Death Rates

In 2021, Africa had the highest cardiovascular disease death rate at 287.12, driven by factors like limited healthcare access, high hypertension rates and inadequate prevention efforts. Asia followed with a rate of 228.47, also impacted by lifestyle risk factors such as poor diet and smoking. Oceania had the lowest rate at 103.01, benefiting from better healthcare, while Europe and the Americas had rates of 178.15 and 151.05, respectively.

Cardiovascular Disease Death Rates by Risk Factor

Europe has the highest death rate from high blood pressure (231.37), followed by Asia (138.54). High cholesterol also significantly impacts both continents, with death rates of 98.45 and 45.59, respectively. Africa faces its greatest risk from high blood pressure (71.80). In the Americas, overweight and high blood pressure contribute to death rates of 30.86 and 97.32, while in Oceania, overweight leads to death rates of 21.37 and high blood pressure to 76.09.

Cardiovascular Disease Death Rates by Disease Type

Ischemic heart disease leads with 669.68 deaths per 100,000 people globally, followed by stroke (447.26). Europe experiences the highest ischemic heart disease death rate 226.36 while Oceania sees a significant burden as well (172.47).
