Global Death Rate from Cardiovascular Diseases

In 2021, Nauru (694.26), the Solomon Islands (566.07) and Uzbekistan (512.86) had the highest cardiovascular death rates, driven by smoking, poor diet and limited healthcare. In contrast, San Marino (60.13), South Korea (60.42) and Japan (69.25) saw the lowest rates, due to strong healthcare systems and healthier lifestyles.

Continents Ranked by Cardiovascular Disease Death Rates

In 2021, Africa had the highest cardiovascular disease death rate at 287.12, driven by factors like limited healthcare access, high hypertension rates and inadequate prevention efforts. Asia followed with a rate of 228.47, also impacted by lifestyle risk factors such as poor diet and smoking. Oceania had the lowest rate at 103.01, benefiting from better healthcare, while Europe and the Americas had rates of 178.15 and 151.05, respectively.
